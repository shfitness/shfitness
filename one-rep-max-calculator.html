Training percentages

Estimates get less reliable above about 10 reps — for best accuracy, use a set of 1-6 reps.

Why estimate instead of testing?

Testing a true 1RM is fatiguing and carries injury risk, especially for beginners. Estimating from a submaximal set gets you a useful number without the wear and tear.

Epley vs. Brzycki

Both formulas estimate 1RM from reps and weight, but weight the relationship slightly differently — we average them for a more balanced estimate.

Using the percentage table

Programs are often written as a percentage of 1RM — knowing your estimated max lets you calculate working weights for any prescribed intensity.

The formulas

# Epley
1RM = weight × (1 + reps / 30)

# Brzycki
1RM = weight × 36 / (37 - reps)

# We show
Displayed 1RM = average of both formulas
