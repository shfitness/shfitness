Spread your protein across meals rather than front- or back-loading it — it helps with both fullness and muscle protein synthesis.

Why protein needs vary

Your protein target depends on your goal, not just your weight. Someone cutting calories needs more protein per kilogram than someone at maintenance, to help protect muscle in a deficit.

Per-meal distribution

Research generally supports spreading protein across 3-6 meals a day, with roughly 0.3-0.4g per kg of bodyweight per meal, to maximise muscle protein synthesis throughout the day.

Is more always better?

Beyond roughly 2.2g/kg, additional protein generally doesn't add extra muscle-building benefit for most people — it's better used for the calories or displaced by carbs and fat.

The formula

# Protein target by goal
General health:            1.2 - 1.6 g / kg bodyweight
Active / maintaining:       1.6 - 2.0 g / kg bodyweight
Fat loss (preserve muscle): 2.0 - 2.4 g / kg bodyweight
Building muscle:            1.8 - 2.2 g / kg bodyweight

# Per meal
Per-meal grams = daily protein ÷ number of meals

These ranges come from sports nutrition research on muscle protein synthesis and retention during a calorie deficit or surplus.
