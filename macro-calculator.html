What are macros?

Macronutrients — protein, carbohydrate and fat — are the three nutrients that make up your daily calories. Getting the split right matters as much as the total.

How we calculate your split

We estimate your BMR with the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, scale it to your activity level for TDEE, adjust calories for your goal, then split the total into grams of protein, carbs and fat.

Why protein comes first

Protein is set as a fixed amount per kilogram of bodyweight before anything else, because it's the macro most tied to preserving and building lean mass — fat and carbs share what's left.

The formula

# Step 1 — BMR (Mifflin-St Jeor)
Men:    BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) - 5 × age + 5
Women:  BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) - 5 × age - 161

# Step 2 — TDEE
TDEE = BMR × activity multiplier

# Step 3 — Goal adjustment
Lose fat:      calories = TDEE - 20%
Maintain:      calories = TDEE
Build muscle:  calories = TDEE + 10%

# Step 4 — Macro split
Protein = 1.8-2.4g / kg bodyweight (goal dependent)
Fat     = 25% of total calories
Carbs   = remaining calories ÷ 4

This is a general-purpose split. If you're following a specific program with different protein or fat targets, use those instead.
