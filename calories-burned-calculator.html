MET-based estimates are a useful ballpark — actual burn varies with intensity, fitness level and individual metabolism.

What a MET value is

A MET (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) compares an activity's energy cost to resting — 1 MET is roughly your resting metabolic rate, and running at 9.8 METs burns about 9.8 times that.

Why bodyweight matters

Heavier bodies burn more calories doing the same activity, since more energy is needed to move more mass — this calculator scales the MET value by your weight.

Using this for weight goals

This is a good rough guide for how much extra activity contributes to your day, but don't rely on it precisely to 'earn back' food — total daily energy balance matters more than any single session.

The formula

# MET formula
Calories/min = (MET × weight(kg) × 3.5) / 200

Total calories = calories/min × duration(minutes)
