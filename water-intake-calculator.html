Why bodyweight matters

Larger bodies have more tissue to hydrate and typically a higher metabolic rate, so total water needs scale with weight rather than being a single fixed number for everyone.

Exercise and sweat losses

Every 30 minutes of exercise adds meaningfully to fluid needs through sweat, so this calculator scales your target up with the amount of exercise you do.

Signs you need more

Dark urine, persistent thirst and headaches can all signal under-hydration — if you notice these regularly, it's worth increasing your intake beyond this estimate.

The formula

# Base need
Base (ml) = weight(kg) × 33

# Exercise addition
Exercise bonus (ml) = exercise minutes × 12

# Climate addition
Hot / humid climate = + 500ml

Total = base + exercise bonus + climate bonus

33ml per kg of bodyweight is a commonly cited general baseline for adults; individual sweat rates vary.
