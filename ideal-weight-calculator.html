These formulas predate modern body composition science — they estimate a healthy weight range, not an optimal physique.

Why three different formulas?

Each formula was developed independently, decades apart, using different populations — comparing all three gives a range rather than relying on a single estimate that might not fit you well.

What these formulas miss

None of them account for muscle mass, frame size or body composition — a heavily muscled athlete will read as 'overweight' by these formulas despite low body fat.

How to use this result

Treat the average as a rough anchor point, not a strict target — body fat percentage and how you look and feel are generally more useful day-to-day guides.

The formulas

# Hamwi (1964)
Men:   48.0kg + 2.7kg per inch over 5ft
Women: 45.5kg + 2.2kg per inch over 5ft

# Devine (1974)
Men:   50.0kg + 2.3kg per inch over 5ft
Women: 45.5kg + 2.3kg per inch over 5ft

# Robinson (1983)
Men:   52.0kg + 1.9kg per inch over 5ft
Women: 49.0kg + 1.7kg per inch over 5ft

All three were originally developed for medical dosing reference, not fitness goals — they're a rough starting anchor at best.
