What each zone is for

Lower zones build an aerobic base and burn a higher percentage of fat, while higher zones improve cardiovascular capacity and speed — most training plans use a mix of both.

Karvonen vs. simple percentage

Adding your resting heart rate (Karvonen method) accounts for your current fitness level, giving more personalised zones than a simple percentage of max heart rate alone.

Finding your resting heart rate

Check your pulse first thing in the morning, before getting out of bed, for the most accurate resting heart rate reading.

The formula

# Max heart rate
Max HR = 220 - age

# Simple zones (% of max HR)
Zone target = Max HR × zone%

# Karvonen method (if resting HR given)
Target = ((Max HR - Resting HR) × zone%) + Resting HR

220 minus age is a widely used estimate; individual max heart rate can vary by 10-15 beats from this formula.
