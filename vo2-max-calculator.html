The Cooper test assumes a maximal, consistent effort for the full 12 minutes on flat ground.

What VO2 max measures

It's the maximum rate your body can use oxygen during intense exercise — a well-established marker of cardiovascular fitness and aerobic capacity.

Why the Cooper test

It's a simple, well-validated field test — run as far as possible in exactly 12 minutes — that correlates well with lab-measured VO2 max without needing special equipment.

Improving your score

VO2 max responds well to consistent aerobic training, especially a mix of steady-state cardio and higher-intensity interval work over weeks to months.
